About

I am a MPhil Criminology student at the University of Cambridge, investigating organisational patterns of tomb robbing in China under the supervision of Professor Justice Tankebe. I hold a First-class BA (Hons) in International Studies from UNNC.

My research interests lie at the intersection of organised crime, transnational criminal networks, and criminal enterprise. My BA dissertation examined methamphetamine trafficking patterns in China using 300+ court documents and quantitative textual analysis.

I have contributed to the UNODC's SHERLOC case law database, served as a Research Assistant at Tsinghua University's Center for Global Studies, and was awarded Best Presentation at a conference. I am particularly interested in diverse themes within organized crime and the application of innovative methodologies to these issues.
